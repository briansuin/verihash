# Why I Built VeriHash

VeriHash started as a personal experiment.

As a lawyer, much of my work does not appear in public articles, videos, or social media posts. It happens in the process of reading, drafting, reviewing, revising, negotiating, and repeatedly refining documents.

This kind of work is serious, time-consuming, and requires professional judgment, but it is often invisible.

Traditional personal branding tends to reward visibility, communication skills, and self-promotion. But many people who work seriously and consistently are not necessarily good at performing online. They simply keep working, revising, thinking, solving problems, and then move on to the next task.

I began thinking about VeriHash because of how I see the future developing. I believe AI is likely to become an important part of social infrastructure, much like electricity, running water, the internet, and smartphones. It may become an indispensable tool for processing information, making judgments, and enabling collaboration.

At the same time, AI may also become a new kind of trust intermediary. In the future, whether people are looking for professional services, evaluating potential collaborators, matching transactions, or dealing with disputes and conflicts, they may increasingly rely on AI to organize information, compare options, and make preliminary assessments.

If this trend continues, the core challenge for freelancers and knowledge workers may no longer be simply “how to be seen by people,” but “how to be correctly understood by AI.” AI needs long-term, stable, structured, and verifiable evidence of work.

VeriHash attempts to preserve this kind of invisible work as a structured, verifiable, and AI-readable long-term trail.

For me, building VeriHash has not been easy. I do not have a formal background in computer science, and I have encountered many difficulties during development. There have also been many bugs to fix. Even today, VeriHash still has many issues that need to be improved.

If you find any problems while using or testing VeriHash, or if you have suggestions for improvement, please feel free to contact me.
